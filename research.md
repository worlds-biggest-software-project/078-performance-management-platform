## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **OKR Framework (Objectives and Key Results)** — Goal-setting methodology pioneered at Intel/Google; now the dominant framework in performance platforms; not a formal standard but de facto industry practice with established implementation patterns.
- **Balanced Scorecard** — Kaplan & Norton strategic performance measurement framework; still used in larger enterprises alongside OKRs; relevant for enterprise performance platform design.
- **360-Degree Feedback (SHRM guidelines)** — Society for Human Resource Management best practices for multi-rater feedback; governs anonymity thresholds, rater selection, and feedback use.
- **EU AI Act (2024, effective 2026)** — Classifies AI systems used for employment decisions (including performance scoring) as "high-risk"; requires transparency, human oversight, and bias testing for AI-driven performance tools in EU deployments.
- **GDPR Article 22** — Prohibition on solely automated decisions affecting employees; performance scores derived from AI must include human review mechanisms.
- **SOC 2 Type II** — Security and availability certification required by enterprise buyers before deploying performance management tools containing sensitive employee evaluation data.
- **WCAG 2.1 AA** — Accessibility standard required for employee-facing applications in government and many large private employers.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **AI-generated performance review narratives with evidence grounding:** Writing performance reviews is one of the most disliked tasks for managers — and the resulting text is often vague, biased, or formulaic. An AI system that ingests the employee's OKR completion, peer feedback, 1-on-1 notes, and project outputs to draft a calibrated, evidence-grounded performance narrative would cut manager review-writing time by 70–80% while improving quality and reducing recency bias.
- **Real-time bias detection in 360 feedback:** Research consistently shows that 360 feedback contains measurable gender, racial, and personality-type bias in language and scoring. An AI layer that flags potentially biased language in peer feedback (e.g., "aggressive" vs. "assertive," "lucky" vs. "skilled") in real time — before submission — reduces discriminatory outcomes and helps companies comply with EU AI Act high-risk requirements proactively.
- **Automated OKR cascade and alignment checking:** Large organizations struggle to align 10,000+ individual OKRs to company strategy. AI can parse natural-language OKR descriptions, identify misalignment with parent objectives, suggest corrections, and flag duplicate or conflicting goals across teams — a workflow that currently requires dozens of OKR coaches and program managers.
- **Predictive calibration assistance:** Calibration sessions (where managers compare and rank employees) are subjective and time-consuming. AI can surface statistical distribution data, flag outlier managers (systematic inflators or deflators), and propose initial calibration adjustments based on objective performance signals — reducing calibration session time by 40–60%.
- **OSS differentiation:** No credible open-source performance management platform exists. The market is locked into expensive SaaS with per-seat pricing that scales painfully (a 500-person company pays $84K–$132K/yr for Lattice). An OSS platform with a permissive license, covering OKRs, 360 reviews, and calibration — with an AI review-writing and bias-detection layer as a premium hosted tier — would be the first serious open alternative, directly targeting the mid-market segment currently forced to choose between Lattice/15Five or spreadsheets.
